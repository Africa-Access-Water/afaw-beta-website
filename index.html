About Us

Empowering Rural Africa Through Solar-Powered Water Solutions

"Water is the foundation of life and opportunity. At Africa Access Water, we believe that when rural communities have access to clean, sustainable water, they can rise beyond poverty and hunger."

Rodney Katongo, Co-Founder & CEO

Africa Access Water (AfAW) is a nonprofit organization based in Zambia, founded in 2019. We implement solar-powered water infrastructure to help communities achieve food security, increase income, and build resilience to climate change. Our programs empower women, youth, and farmers across Zambia and beyond.

Our Core Purpose

Vision, Mission & Strategic Objectives

Our Vision

African communities developed through solar-powered water.

Our Mission

To equip rural communities in Zambia and Africa with solar-powered water infrastructure and systems for productive use, food security, and socio-economic development.

Our Objectives
  1. Provide solar-powered water infrastructure to improve food security and socio-economic development.
  2. Empower women and youth in agriculture and entrepreneurship.
  3. Advance water economics through research, innovation, and policy advocacy.

What We Do

Empowering Communities Through Sustainable Water Solutions

Water Infrastructure

We design and implement solar-powered water systems for drinking, farming, and livestock in rural Zambia.

Agricultural Productivity

We promote food security and income generation by supporting irrigated farming, aquaculture, and agribusiness models.

Research & Advocacy

We conduct impact assessments and drive policy change through dialogues on the Water-Energy-Agriculture nexus.

Join Us in Making a Difference

Two sites in Mphande and Rufunsa communities have been earmark to install Solar powered water for productive use- all year irrigation, drinking and animals. The two site will help at least 2,300 people with clean drinkning water, at 100 smale-scale farmers/ households (1000 people) growing different crops with increased income and food secure, 1,000 animals. We are looking for US$93,000 for the two sites.
